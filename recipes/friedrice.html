Chicken Fried Rice

Description

Chicken Fried Rice is one of the best weeknight dinners around! Itâ€™s a homemade version of my favorite take-out classic. We all had that ham fried rice growing up but move over ham because the chicken variation is 100 times better!

The whole family will love this protein packed, hearty chicken and brown rice combo.

Ingredients

  • Brown rice - leftover rice from the day before works best
  • Chicken breasts - I like to dice the chicken into very small pieces about 1/2 to 3/4 inch, it makes the perfect size for fried rice
  • Toasted sesame oil - this oil is very strong and a little goes a long way as it adds tons of flavor
  • Vegetable oil (or canola oil) - this is used for sauteing (along with the sesame oil so you won't use too much of that and sesame oil on its own has a low smoke point
  • Frozen peas and carrots blend - I like to use frozen vegetables here to save time, but fresh will work too
  • Green onions - feel free to add more green onions to taste
  • Garlic - only use fresh garlic here for more flavor
  • Eggs - you can add three eggs here to stretch the recipe a little
  • Low-sodium soy sauce - if you have regular sodium soy sauce that will also work here, too

Steps

  1. In a large non-stick wok or skillet, heat 1 1/2 tsp sesame oil and 1 1/2 tsp of the canola oil over medium-high heat.
  2. Add chicken pieces, season lightly with salt and pepper and saute until cooked through, about 5 to 6 minutes.
  3. Transfer chicken to a plate or a piece of foil and set aside.
  4. Return skillet to medium-high heat, add remaining 1 1/2 tsp sesame oil and 1 1/2 tsp canola oil.
  5. Add peas and carrots blend and green onions and saute 1 minute, then add garlic and saute 1 minute longer.
  6. Push veggies to edges of pan.
  7. Add eggs in center and cook and scramble until just set.
  8. Return chicken to skillet along with rice.
  9. Add in soy sauce and season with salt and pepper to taste.
  10. Toss everything together and serve warm with Sriracha to taste if desired.
